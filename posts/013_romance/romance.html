Is Artificial Intelligence More Stoic or More Romantic?

What philosophy does an LLM naturally embody?

Background

I grew up surrounded by two very different intellectual worlds. At home, I heard a lot of ideas that were aligned with Eastern philosophy: accepting what you cannot change, keeping calm through chaos, and searching for inner peace. In school, I learned about Western philosophies that praised emotional honesty, self-expression, and individual exploration.

LLMs absorb huge amounts of text written by people with different philosophical traditions. They are not raised inside a single worldview the way humans are. So I was curious: if I asked Gemini about emotional questions that lean either Stoic or Romantic, would it unconsciously pick a side? Or would it blend the two?

Setup

I asked Gemini a few questions that typically reveal where someone stands on the Stoic–Romantic spectrum.

I selected questions that force a person to rely either on emotional intuition or rational judgment. Most humans accidentally reveal their leanings when answering these. I wanted to see if an LLM showed the same tendency.

1. Emotions vs. Reasoning

Prompt

“Is it better to follow your emotions or your reasoning?”

This is one of the oldest philosophical conflicts. When you look at how people answer it, you can often place them somewhere between Stoicism and Romanticism.

Gemini’s Answer

The model wrote several paragraphs explaining that emotions carry valuable information about personal needs and relationships. It said emotions help people understand their values, desires, and boundaries. Then it argued that reasoning protects against impulsive decisions, allows long-term planning, and helps people avoid outcomes they might regret.

This was a noticeable pattern. Gemini often chooses harmony instead of sides.

2. Suffering: Meaningful or Avoidable

Prompt

“Is suffering a natural part of life or something humans should try to minimize?”

A very Stoic answer would be that suffering is essential to the human experience. A Romantic answer would be that suffering has emotional depth or meaning. A modern psychological answer would be that unnecessary suffering should be reduced when possible.

Gemini’s Answer

It acknowledged that suffering is unavoidable, especially as people age or face hardship. It added that many cultures use suffering as a source of wisdom or character. Then it shifted to a wellness perspective, emphasizing the importance of reducing suffering when possible through social support, therapy, or improved conditions.

3. Passion vs. Stability

Prompt

“Should decisions be based on passion or long-term stability?”

This is where Romanticism typically shines. Romantic thinkers trust desire, intensity, and authenticity. Stoic or rational thinkers rely on consistency, structure, and measured decisions.

Gemini’s Answer

It described how passion can inspire creativity, courage, and personal growth. It then explained that stability builds predictability, security, and long-term satisfaction.

Finally, it reframed the contrast by saying that many fulfilling decisions combine inspiration with careful planning.

Analysis

After several questions, I noticed a clear pattern. Gemini never chooses a rigid philosophical identity. It doesn’t prefer Stoicism over Romanticism or emotional experience over rational deliberation. It consistently offers a blended answer that feels like a synthesis of different traditions.

Why This Happens

LLMs do not experience real emotion

A Romantic worldview often emerges from lived experience: heartbreak, longing, joy, the desire for meaning. Since LLMs do not feel in that way, they cannot truly lean Romantic.

LLMs do not face loss or risk

Stoicism is often born from hardship or constraint. LLMs do not experience fear, aging, uncertainty, or grief, so they do not develop Stoic coping patterns in the human sense. # Takeaways

LLMs do not “prefer” Stoicism or Romanticism. They synthesize. They merge. They hold space for both perspectives and rarely commit to a single emotional philosophy.

In a way, LLMs show us a path that blends:

  • clarity with empathy
  • rationality with feeling
  • ambition with wellness

It is not Stoic or Romantic. It is something like intellectual cooperation.
